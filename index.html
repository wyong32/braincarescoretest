Frequently asked questions

Brain Care Score FAQ

What is a good Brain Care Score?

Yes, a higher score is better. The maximum possible Brain Care Score (BCS) is 21 points. According to research by the McCance Center, a higher score is associated with a lower risk of developing dementia and stroke later in life.

Studies suggest that even a 5-point improvement in your score can significantly reduce the risk of these neurological conditions. However, "good" is relative to your starting point; the goal is continuous improvement in your daily habits.

What does a Brain Care Score of 18 mean?

A score of 18 out of 21 is considered excellent and indicates that you are currently managing your brain health risk factors very well. It means you are likely excelling in most of the three core categories: Physical metrics (like blood pressure), Lifestyle choices (like sleep and diet), and Social-Emotional well-being.

Please note: This score is a general indicator, not a clinical diagnosis. Always discuss your health metrics with a primary care physician.

How is the Brain Care Score calculated?

The BCS is a 21-point system that evaluates 12 modifiable risk factors divided into three main categories:

Physical (0-8 points): Blood pressure, blood sugar (HbA1c), cholesterol, and BMI.

Lifestyle (0-10 points): Diet/nutrition, alcohol consumption, smoking habits, aerobic activity, and sleep quality.

Social Emotional (0-3 points): Stress management, relationships, and having a sense of purpose in life.

Is this online test official?

No. This website provides an independent, unofficial, and interactive calculator designed to make the official McCance Center worksheet easier to complete and print. We are not affiliated with Massachusetts General Hospital. For clinical decisions, please use the official PDF and consult your doctor.

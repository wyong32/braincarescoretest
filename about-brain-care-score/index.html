About the McCance Brain Care Score (BCS)

The McCance Brain Care Score was developed by the McCance Center for Brain Health at Massachusetts General Hospital. It is designed to help people and clinicians review modifiable physical, lifestyle, and social-emotional contributors to brain health.

What the score measures

The BCS uses a 0-21 point framework across 12 domains. Physical domains include blood pressure, blood sugar, cholesterol, and BMI. Lifestyle domains include nutrition, alcohol, smoking, aerobic activity, and sleep. Social-emotional domains include stress, relationships, and meaning in life.

Scientific purpose

Mass General describes the score as a way to support better brain care and reduce risk factors associated with dementia, stroke, depression, and other brain diseases. A higher score indicates better current brain care in the official framework.

Adult BMI Categories

BMI categories are commonly used as a screening reference for adults. They do not diagnose health status, body composition, or disease risk for an individual person.

BMI range Common category Brain Care Score option
Below 18.5 Underweight Lower than 18.5 kg/m2
18.5 to 24.9 Healthy weight range 18.5-25 kg/m2
25.0 to 29.9 Overweight range 25-29.9 kg/m2
30.0 or higher Obesity range Greater than 30 kg/m2

BMI Formula

US units

BMI = 703 x weight(lb) / height(in)^2

Metric units

BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)^2

What BMI Can and Cannot Tell You

BMI is simple and useful for screening because it uses only height and weight. However, it does not directly measure body fat, muscle mass, waist circumference, fitness, age-related body changes, or medical history.

If your BMI result is unexpected, or if you have questions about weight, nutrition, medication, or chronic conditions, discuss it with a qualified health professional.
